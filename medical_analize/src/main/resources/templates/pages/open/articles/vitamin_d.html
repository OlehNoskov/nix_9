Vitamin D

Vitamin D is a fat-soluble vitamin that exists in two forms, D2 and D3.

D3 differs from other vitamins in that it is formed under the skin when exposed to ultraviolet radiation.

D2 comes into the body with food and supplements, the largest amount contains:

🐟 Salmon

🐟 Sardines and other fatty fish

🐟 Herring

🐟 Canned Tuna

🐟 Cod liver oil

🥚 Egg yolks

🍄 Mushrooms

🍶 Cow's milk

🥛 Soy milk

🍊 Orange Juice

💊 Vitamin D and Calcium

Studies have shown that vitamin D deficiency is widespread throughout the world, but mostly only in those people who do not eat enough foods containing it and spend very little time in the sun.

Vitamin D deficiency can affect the immune system, as this vitamin plays the role of an immunomodulator, strengthening innate immunity by increasing the production and secretion of antimicrobial proteins that increase the protective functions of the body's mucous membranes.

Also, a lack of vitamin D plays an important role in the occurrence of both pathology of the skeletal system, and many different socially significant chronic diseases, including type 2 diabetes mellitus, cardiovascular disease, certain cancers, autoimmune diseases, and infectious diseases, and is also associated with increased mortality.

In addition, a recent WHO study documented the protective effect of vitamin D on respiratory infections.

Vitamin content norm:

✔️30-100 ng / ml, in insufficient content - from 10-30 ng / ml, a level below 10 ng / ml indicates a deficiency.
